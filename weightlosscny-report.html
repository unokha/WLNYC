Four confirmed patterns

Two live pages intermittently 404

/hormone-replacement-therapy and /our-practice each show up both as normal pageviews and as "Page not found" on the exact same URL within the same 90 days. That's not a dead link — it's the live page failing some of the time. Worth a developer look at caching, CDN rules, or server rewrites before doing any redirect work here.

Missing trailing slash may be breaking conversion tracking

/thank-you (no slash) 404s 39 times while /thank-you/ (with slash) is the real, working page. If any ad platform, email template, or CRM automation is set up to send people to the no-slash version, those conversions — and any pixel that fires on that page — are being lost silently.

A renamed post lost its redirect

/what-causes-an-increase-in-abdominal-fat-after-menopause (404, 28 views) is near-identical in topic to the live /what-causes-an-increase-in-belly-fat-after-menopause/ (38 views) — looks like the post was renamed and the old slug was never redirected.

A whole legacy URL structure was never redirected

The bulk of the 229 broken paths share a handful of old prefixes (/lifestyle/, /patient-testimonials/, /healthy-eating-menu/, /medical-weight-loss-guide/) plus short nav paths like /products, /solutions, /blog, /faq. This is the signature of a past site migration or redesign where old URLs were never bulk-redirected to their new equivalents.
